Evan Peters

Evan Thomas Peters (born January 20, 1987) is an American actor.
He is best known for his work in the FX horror anthology series American Horror Story,
having appeared in multiple seasons since 2011.
Peters has received critical acclaim for his performances and several award nominations,
including a Critics' Choice Television Award.

He began acting in television series and films in the mid-2000s,
with roles in shows like *Phil of the Future* and movies such as *Never Back Down* (2008).
Peters gained widespread recognition for his collaborations with Ryan Murphy,
especially in *American Horror Story*, portraying a variety of unique characters over the years.

Early life and education

Evan Peters was born in St. Louis, Missouri, on January 20, 1987,
the son of Julie (née Cain) and Robert Peters.
He grew up in a Catholic family and has several siblings.
Peters showed interest in acting at a young age and participated in local theater productions.

He attended the Notre Dame High School in St. Louis before moving to Los Angeles to pursue acting.
Early in his career, he balanced school and acting roles, gradually building his presence in television and film.
This led to his breakthrough role in *American Horror Story*.
